What is Anime?

Anime is a Japanese style of animation that can range from full-length movies, television series, and specials, as well as video games and comic books. Anime includes hand-drawn or computer-generated animation. The word "anime" is the Japanese word for "animation". The history of anime can be traced back to 1894 when a Japanese artist by the name of Katsuzo Hirata created his first illustrated story called "Takane no hana" which later became an animated short in 1917. The first anime production was made in 1917 by Kazuo Oga with the help of Seiichi Hayashi. It was an animated short called “Chikara to Onna no Yo no Naka” which translates into "Within the World of Power and Women". It is an incredibly popular form of entertainment across the world, with many people enjoying this form of art. As well as being entertaining, anime can also offer valuable insights into different cultures.
